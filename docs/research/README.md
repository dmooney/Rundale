# Parish Research Collection

Reference hub for 23 research documents covering life in 1820s Ireland, written to support world-building in Parish -- an interactive fiction game set in Kilteevan, County Roscommon, with scope to expand across all of Ireland.

These documents provide the historical, cultural, and material foundations for NPC dialogue, location descriptions, game mechanics, and narrative design. Each document includes a dedicated "Game Design Implications" section translating research into actionable design guidance.

Supporting: [Education & Literacy](education-literacy.md), [Mythology & Folklore](mythology-folklore.md), [Demographics & Social Structure](demographics-social-structure.md), [Family Life](family-life.md)

NPCs need bilingual name forms, class-appropriate speech registers, religious references woven into conversation, and literacy levels that determine how they receive and relay information. ## Key Themes Across Documents

Several threads run through multiple documents, connecting them into a coherent picture of 1820s Ireland:

**The landlord-tenant relationship** is the single most pervasive theme, appearing in [demographics](demographics-social-structure.md), [economy](economy-trade.md), [farming](farming-agriculture.md), [law](law-governance.md), [politics](politics-movements.md), [crime](crime-secret-societies.md), [architecture](architecture-housing.md), and [recent history](recent-history-pre1820.md). Rent, eviction, the agent, and the middleman system touch every aspect of life.

**The Irish language under pressure** surfaces in [language](irish-language.md), [education](education-literacy.md), [names](names-naming-conventions.md), [music](music-entertainment.md), and [mythology](mythology-folklore.md). The bilingual world is not stable -- English is advancing through commerce, law, and parental aspiration, while Irish remains the language of home, story, and identity.

**Poverty and its markers** connect [food](food-drink.md), [clothing](clothing-textiles.md), [architecture](architecture-housing.md), [economy](economy-trade.md), [medicine](medicine-health.md), and [demographics](demographics-social-structure.md). What you eat, what you wear, whether you have shoes, the state of your cabin, and whether you can afford a doctor all communicate the same reality of material deprivation.

**The Catholic Church as community anchor** threads through [religion](religion-spirituality.md), [politics](politics-movements.md), [education](education-literacy.md), [culture](culture-daily-life.md), and [family life](family-life.md). The parish priest, the Sunday Mass, the chapel-as-political-organising-site, and the stations system make the Church inseparable from daily social life.

**Pre-Christian belief coexisting with Catholicism** links [mythology](mythology-folklore.md), [religion](religion-spirituality.md), [medicine](medicine-health.md), and [culture](culture-daily-life.md). Fairy forts, holy wells, pisheogs, the bean feasa, and the four seasonal festivals all operate within a worldview that sees no contradiction between the rosary and the fairy thorn.

**Seasonal rhythms governing all life** appear in [farming](farming-agriculture.md), [culture](culture-daily-life.md), [food](food-drink.md), [clothing](clothing-textiles.md), [technology](technology-crafts.md), [flora/fauna](flora-fauna-landscape.md), [music](music-entertainment.md), and [mythology](mythology-folklore.md). The farming calendar, the meal months, seasonal migration, crossroads dances in summer, storytelling in winter -- time structures everything.

**The shadow of 1798** haunts [recent history](recent-history-pre1820.md), [politics](politics-movements.md), [crime](crime-secret-societies.md), and [law](law-governance.md). The rebellion, the repression, the Act of Union, and the broken promise of Emancipation form the political memory that shapes how NPCs understand authority, risk, and resistance.

**The collapse of cottage industry** connects [clothing](clothing-textiles.md), [technology](technology-crafts.md), and [economy](economy-trade.md). English factory cloth is destroying the domestic spinning and weaving economy, removing one of the few non-agricultural income sources and deepening dependence on the potato.

## Suggested Reading Order

For someone new to the project, this path moves from the big picture to the specific:

1. **[Demographics & Social Structure](demographics-social-structure.md)** -- Start here. This is the map of the world: who lives in it, how power works, and where Kilteevan fits.

2. **[Culture & Daily Life](culture-daily-life.md)** -- The texture of ordinary days: routines, visiting, hospitality, fairs, wakes, and the seasonal calendar. This is what the player will actually experience.

3. **[Irish Language](irish-language.md)** -- The bilingual reality that shapes every conversation in the game.

4. **[Economy & Trade](economy-trade.md)** -- The economic pressures that drive NPC anxiety and conflict.

5. **[Religion & Spirituality](religion-spirituality.md)** -- The spiritual and institutional framework of community life.

6. **[Mythology & Folklore](mythology-folklore.md)** -- The fairy faith and pre-Christian belief layer that gives the world its uncanny depth.

7. **[Recent History (Pre-1820)](recent-history-pre1820.md)** -- What NPCs remember. The 1798 rebellion and its aftermath shape everything.

8. **[Forthcoming Decades](forthcoming-decades.md)** -- What the designers know but the characters do not. Essential for foreshadowing and dramatic irony.

After these eight, branch into whichever documents are most relevant to what you are building:

- Working on NPCs? Read [names](names-naming-conventions.md), [family life](family-life.md), [education](education-literacy.md)
- Working on locations? Read [architecture](architecture-housing.md), [flora/fauna](flora-fauna-landscape.md), [transportation](transportation.md)
- Working on game events? Read [farming](farming-agriculture.md), [music](music-entertainment.md), [politics](politics-movements.md)
- Working on conflict systems? Read [crime](crime-secret-societies.md), [law](law-governance.md), [politics](politics-movements.md)
- Working on atmosphere? Read [food](food-drink.md), [clothing](clothing-textiles.md), [medicine](medicine-health.md), [technology](technology-crafts.md)
