# Ambient Sound Sources — Royalty-Free Audio for Parish

## Research Document for Parish (Kilteevan, County Roscommon)

This document catalogs royalty-free and Creative Commons audio sources for the ambient sound system. All sounds must be period-plausible for 1820s rural Ireland — no engines, electronics, or modern machinery. Preferred formats: OGG Vorbis or WAV. Preferred licenses: CC0 (public domain) or CC-BY (attribution only).

---

## Sound Categories & Sources

### 1. Irish Traditional Music (Pub / Crossroads Dances)

The fiddle was the most common instrument in 1820s rural Ireland. Uilleann pipes were rarer but prestigious. Sean-nós singing was the vocal tradition. See `docs/research/music-entertainment.md` for full historical context.

**What we need:**
- Fiddle reels and jigs (loopable, 30–60s clips)
- Uilleann pipe airs (rare event sound)
- Sean-nós singing (solo unaccompanied voice)
- General "session" ambience (multiple instruments, informal)

**Sources:**

| Source | License | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| [Free Music Archive — Celtic genre](https://freemusicarchive.org/genre/Celtic) | Varies (CC-BY, CC0) | Large catalog of Celtic/Irish traditional music. Check individual track licenses. Best source for full tunes. |
| [Silverman Sound — "Fiddle De Dee"](https://www.silvermansound.com/free-music/fiddle-de-dee) | CC-BY 4.0 | Fiddle and penny whistle melody with bodhran, bouzouki, uilleann pipes. Energetic Irish folk. Good pub atmosphere. |
| [Free-Stock-Music.com — Celtic](https://www.free-stock-music.com/celtic.html) | CC-BY | Fiddle, flute, and pipe tracks. Most require attribution. |
| [Chosic — Celtic Background Music](https://www.chosic.com/free-music/celtic/) | Varies (CC-BY, Public Domain) | Includes traditional tunes: "Jig Of Slurs," "Dublin Reel," "The Butterfly." Some public domain (no credit needed). |
| [Pond5 — Traditional Irish](https://www.pond5.com/royalty-free-music/tag/traditional-irish/) | Paid ($5+) | 3,300+ clips. Fallback if free sources lack quality. |

**Period accuracy notes:**
- Bodhran is debatable for 1820s (see music-entertainment.md [4]). Tracks featuring prominent bodhran are acceptable but not ideal.
- Avoid tracks with guitar, piano, accordion, or any amplified instrument.
- Solo fiddle recordings are most period-accurate.

### 2. Church Bells

Church bells would have been the most far-carrying sound in the parish, audible from every location. Bells toll for: Angelus (morning, noon, evening), Sunday Mass, funerals, weddings, and alarm/fire.

**What we need:**

- Bell sequence (peal for Sunday/events)
- Distant bells (attenuated, for far locations)

## Asset Preparation Guidelines

1. **Format**: Convert all assets to OGG Vorbis (`.ogg`) at 44.1kHz, mono or stereo. OGG is patent-free, well-compressed, and natively supported by rodio.
2. **Looping**: Ambient layers (water, wind, pub murmur, fire) should be seamlessly loopable. Trim to clean loop points.
3. **Duration**: Ambient loops: 15–60 seconds. Event sounds (bell toll, rooster crow): natural duration.
4. **Normalization**: Normalize all assets to -3dB peak. The game's volume system handles relative mixing.
5. **File size budget**: Target <20 MB total for the full audio catalog. OGG at quality 3 (~96 kbps) gives good results for ambient audio.
6. **Naming convention**: `{category}/{subcategory}_{variant}.ogg` — e.g., `church/bell_toll_close.ogg`, `farm/rooster_crow_01.ogg`.

## Fact-Check Notes

[1] **Freesound.org licensing verified.** Freesound allows uploading under CC0, CC-BY, and CC-BY-NC licenses. Each sound's license is displayed on its page. The site has been operating since 2005 under the Music Technology Group at Pompeu Fabra University.

[2] **Pixabay License verified.** Pixabay's license permits free use for commercial and non-commercial purposes without attribution. Content cannot be sold as-is on stock media platforms. This is compatible with game distribution.

[3] **rodio OGG support verified.** rodio supports OGG Vorbis decoding via the `vorbis` feature (enabled by default). Also supports WAV, MP3 (via `minimp3`), and FLAC.
